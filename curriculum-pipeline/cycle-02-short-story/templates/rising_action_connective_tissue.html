What Holds the Rising Action Together?
A reference guide for story builders

In the rising action, your character keeps pushing toward their goal — but nothing is working. Every event needs to connect to the one before it. Here are seven things that can serve as the thread running through your rising action:

🧊 The Antagonist
A person, force, or system that keeps getting in the way — not once, but again and again.
Draco keeps humiliating Harry. Voldemort's influence spreads across every obstacle.
🧠 The MC's Flaw
Your MC keeps failing for the same reason — they haven't changed yet. Their weakness shapes every decision.
Harry's impulsiveness makes each new obstacle worse than it has to be.
🔍 The Central Mystery
A question the reader — and MC — can't stop thinking about. Each event reveals a clue or deepens the mystery.
What is in the third-floor corridor? What was Voldemort after? Each chapter inches closer.
🤝 A Key Relationship
A friendship, rivalry, or alliance that grows or breaks under pressure. The relationship's state mirrors the story's tension.
Harry, Ron, and Hermione's friendship is tested, broken, and rebuilt as their situation gets more dangerous.
⏳ A Ticking Clock
A deadline or shrinking window that makes every failure feel more costly. Each event eats away at the time left.
The Sorcerer's Stone is about to be stolen — and each failed attempt to act brings Voldemort closer to getting it.
🔗 Cause and Effect
One event causes the next. Your MC's response to each problem creates a new, bigger problem.
Harry gets detention → misses Quidditch practice → loses the match → is banned from flying → can no longer pursue clues.
🏛️ A Recurring Place or System
The same location or institution keeps showing up — but it means something different each time. The MC's relationship to it changes as the stakes rise.
Hogwarts starts as a safe haven. By the end of the rising action, every corridor feels dangerous — the school itself has become part of the threat.
Your story doesn't need all seven. Pick one or two that fit your MC's situation and let them run through your whole rising action. Ask yourself: what is the thread my reader will be following?
