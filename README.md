## HTSB Standards Coverage

**Standard 1: Learner Development**
The teacher designs and implements developmentally appropriate and challenging learning
experiences that account for individual patterns of growth across cognitive, linguistic,
social, emotional, and physical areas.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Five-assignment sequence scaffolded
  from single-paragraph evidence writing to a full argument essay, each task building
  directly on the last to match students' developing capacity
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Multi-week drafting cycle structured
  around a 22-step narrative framework, moving students through one manageable stage at
  a time

---

**Standard 2: Learning Differences**
The teacher uses understanding of individual differences and diverse cultures to ensure
inclusive learning environments that enable each learner to meet high standards.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Two differentiated pathways
  documented in full: Standard and ELL versions of every assignment, with scaffolded
  sentence frames, pre-selected quotes, a dedicated pre-writing task (Gathering Quotes),
  and one-on-one instructional support for the ELL student
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Templates generated with cohort-specific
  content and designed to be reprinted with modifications for individual student needs

---

**Standard 3: Learning Environments**
The teacher creates environments that support individual and collaborative learning,
positive social interaction, active engagement, and self-motivation.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Escape room activity gamified essay
  structure: partner pairs unlocked a combination lock by correctly sequencing the twelve
  components of an argument essay
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Published author visits twice per week to
  read drafts and work directly with students; hand-drawn classroom posters provide
  persistent visual scaffolds for story structure and character motivation

---

**Standard 4: Content Knowledge**
The teacher understands the central concepts and structures of the discipline and creates
learning experiences that make these accessible and meaningful.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Literary analysis of *Frankenstein*
  (Gris Grimly adaptation); argument essay structure including hook, thesis,
  counterargument, body paragraphs with evidence and analysis, rebuttal, and conclusion
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Short story craft across structure,
  scene-level writing, and voice; 22-step narrative framework contributed by a published
  author

---

**Standard 5: Application of Content**
The teacher connects concepts and uses differing perspectives to engage learners in
critical thinking, creativity, and collaborative problem solving related to authentic
local and global issues.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Central question (*Is the Creature
  human or not?*) required students to take and defend a position on identity and
  humanity, with the ELL student producing a dual-sided thesis that the question cannot
  be resolved
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Students write original stories from
  scratch, applying structural knowledge to self-generated creative problems

---

**Standard 6: Assessment**
The teacher understands and uses multiple methods of assessment to engage learners in
their own growth, to monitor progress, and to guide instructional decision making.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Rubric-based scoring documented at
  each stage; written teacher feedback for each student on each assignment; skill
  callbacks in the final essay analysis trace what each student carried forward from
  prior assessments
- `nwea-agent`: Conversational tool for querying anonymized MAP Growth data to support
  data-driven instructional conversations with administrators
- `nwea-explorer`: Terminal viewer for browsing MAP Growth RIT scores, sub-goal
  breakdowns, and Fall-to-Winter growth by grade and teacher

---

**Standard 7: Planning for Instruction**
The teacher plans instruction that supports every student in meeting rigorous learning
goals by drawing on content knowledge, curriculum, cross-disciplinary skills, pedagogy,
and community context.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Five-assignment instructional arc
  planned and sequenced to address diagnosed gaps (rebuttal lesson added after ELL
  scored 0/5 on rebuttal in the gathering quotes rubric)
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Unit sequenced in collaboration with a
  published author; each week's worksheet targets one discrete skill (plan, show, or
  voice) to prevent conflation of skill targets

---

**Standard 8: Instructional Strategies**
The teacher uses a variety of instructional strategies to encourage deep understanding
and build skills to apply knowledge in meaningful ways.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Strategies include gamification
  (escape room), scaffolded pre-writing (sentence frames, gathering quotes table),
  direct notebook instruction (rebuttal formula), and individualized verbal feedback
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Three-pass drafting structure (sequence,
  show, voice); connective tissue reference guide with color-coded cards; expert author
  modeling and feedback; visual classroom posters as low-tech persistent scaffolds

---

**Standard 9: Professional Learning and Ethical Practice**
The teacher engages in ongoing professional learning and uses evidence to continually
evaluate practice, particularly the effects of choices and actions on learners and the
broader community.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-01-argument-essay`: Documented teacher feedback and
  instructional adjustments in response to observed student performance at each stage
- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: First unit built in full accordance with
  the teaching philosophy documented in `philosophy/manifesto.md`; reflects on the
  ethical use of AI as an invisible production tool under teacher control
- `nwea-agent`: Teacher-built data tool demonstrating applied professional learning in
  software development and responsible AI use
- `nwea-explorer`: Personal data analysis tool built to support professional
  preparation for data conversations

---

**Standard 10: Leadership and Collaboration**
The teacher seeks leadership roles and collaborates with learners, families, colleagues,
and community members to ensure learner growth and advance the profession.

- `curriculum-pipeline/cycle-02-short-story`: Collaboration with a published Big Island
  author who visits twice per week and contributed the unit's core 22-step narrative
  framework; her hand-drawn classroom materials are documented as part of the unit record
